The strategic information system planning process includes 5 phases: strategic business planning, information systems assessment, information system vision, information system guidelines, and strategic initiatives.
1. Strategic business planning: The general and functional managers have to propose the plan of the company’s mission, future direction, and target. This business plan is the prerequisite information system planning.
2. Information systems assessment: This is the process that starting to evaluate whether the existing resource is enough or not to execute the information system plan.
3. Information systems vision: This is a concise statement to specify the role of the information system play in the organization.
4. Information system guidelines: This provides a bunch of rules that allow employees to be aligned with the company’s aim.
5. Strategic Initiatives: This gives the long-term proposal of the new system and new project.
